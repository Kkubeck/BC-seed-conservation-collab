Methodologies

Dormancy Breaking & Yield Optimization

Dormancy Classification

To standardize treatment protocols, the Nursery utilizes the Nikolaeva-Baskin Classification System. Unlike descriptive systems, this approach targets the specific mechanism preventing germination.

Code Type Mechanism
ND No Dormancy Embryo is mature and ready to germinate.
PY Physical Water-impermeable coat prevents uptake.
PD Physiological Internal chemical factors inhibit germination.
MD Morphological Embryo is underdeveloped at dispersal.
MPD Morphophysiological Combination of underdeveloped embryo and physiological inhibition.

Seed Coat Treatments (PY)

Treatments targeting Physical Dormancy (PY) involve modifying the seed coat to allow water and oxygen penetration.

Mechanical Scarification

Physically damaging the seed coat.

  • Sandpaper Abrasion: Effective for seeds large enough to hold. Note: Change sandpaper regularly as debris accumulates.
  • Nicking: Using a scalpel to breach the coat. Target: Water gaps (natural weak points). Common Usage: Large legumes like Lupinus.
  • Tumblers: For large batches, industrial rock tumblers with coarse grit are used (See species profiles for duration).

Chemical Scarification

Using corrosive agents to etch the coat.

Safety First

Sulfuric Acid (\(H_2SO_4\)): The primary chemical scarification agent at the Nursery due to efficiency. Used for Arctostaphylos. Requires strict safety protocols.

  • Hydrogen Peroxide (\(H_2O_2\)): Soaking in dilute solution. May also break down inhibitors affecting PD.
  • Ethanol: Historic usage for surface disinfection and inhibitor breakdown.

Heat Treatments

Mimicking wildfire conditions or thermal shock.

  • Heat Shock: Exposure to 80-100°C water. Nursery Protocol: Beaker of boiling water with constant stirring (e.g., Ceanothus).
  • Hot Water Soak: Immersion in 70-90°C water, then allowed to cool.
  • Plasma Treatment: A research-grade technique using plasma streams to ‘pit’ the seed coat and surface sterilize. (See Project Scope).

Physiological Treatments (PD/MPD)

Treatments addressing internal chemical dormancy or embryo development.

Hydropriming

Controlled hydration to activate metabolic processes without full germination.

  1. Cold Water Soak: 10-15°C for >12 hours. Softens coats and hydrates.
  2. Warm Water Soak: 25-40°C. Promotes absorption in species requiring moderate heat.
  3. Constant Water Drip: Continuous supply to wash away surface inhibitors.

Growth Regulators

Hormonal applications to stimulate embryo growth.

  • Smoke Primer: Infused water containing karrikins. Target: Fire-adapted species (California chaparral, Mediterranean).
  • Gibberellic Acid (\(GA_3\)): Stimulates embryo growth for deep dormancy. Warning: Monitor concentration to avoid adverse seedling effects.
  • Potassium Nitrate (\(KNO_3\)): 0.2-0.3% soak. Stimulates metabolic activity.

Osmotic Priming

Research Application: Soaking in low water potential solutions (PEG or Salts) to simulate drought/stress. * Note: Rarely used at the Nursery due to limited need for PEG6000 applications.

Environmental Stratification

Simulating seasonal cycles to break complex dormancy.

Nursery Locations
  • Cold Stratification (1-5°C): Production House (winter) or Header House Refrigerator.
  • Warm Stratification (15-25°C): Heat bench in 7A (Feb–June) or Production House Seed Table (June–Oct).
  • Alternating Temps: Production House North Cold Frames (bottom heat cycled on/off).

Seedling Support & Ecology

treatments applied at sowing or post-germination to ensure establishment.

Inoculants

  • Mycorrhizal: Fungi introduced to enhance nutrient uptake (Phosphorus). Crucial for Ericaceae.
  • Bacterial: Rhizobium introduction for nitrogen-fixing legumes.

Ecological Interactions

  • Hemiparasite Co-cultivation: Simultaneous germination of Castilleja spp. with native hosts to form haustorial connections.
  • Allelopathy Mitigation: Using activated charcoal or single-cell sowing to prevent infraspecific chemical warfare (e.g., Arctostaphylos).
